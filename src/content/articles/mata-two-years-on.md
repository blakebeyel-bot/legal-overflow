*Mata v. Avianca* landed in June 2023 as a fable — two attorneys fined $5,000 each for filing a brief riddled with hallucinated citations. Two years later, it has become something else: the precedent that every BigLaw general counsel points to when rolling out mandatory AI-use policy.

## What's in a modern citation-verification pipeline

The compliance programs that emerged post-*Mata* follow a common architecture:
- Extraction (every citation pulled from the draft)
- Cross-check (Westlaw, Lexis, CourtListener, and official reporters)
- Pin-cite validation (quotations matched against source text)
- Human sign-off (recorded, timestamped, reviewable)

At 41 of the AmLaw 100, this pipeline runs automatically as part of the doc-management system.

## What's still getting through

Three categories of errors survive the pipeline: misquoted-but-real cases, cases that exist but stand for the opposite proposition cited, and citations to unpublished opinions that look real but were misattributed. The last one is the hardest.
